Key Finding

The Adoption Surge

Between 2023 and 2025, AI coding tool adoption among developers accelerated from 43.8% to 78.5% according to Stack Overflow's annual developer survey. That's a 34.7 percentage-point jump in just two years — a pace that outstrips almost every prior developer tooling shift. The inflection point was 2024, when ChatGPT and Copilot crossed from novelty to daily infrastructure.

Source: Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2023, 2024, 2025
Key Finding

The Trust Paradox

Despite near-universal adoption, trust remains strikingly low. 46% of developers distrust AI-generated output, and only 3% express high trust. Developers are using these tools constantly while simultaneously doubting what they produce. The gap between usage and trust is the defining tension of the current era — teams ship AI-assisted code faster but spend growing effort on review and verification.

Source: Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2025
Key Finding

The Agentic Frontier

Agentic AI — tools that autonomously plan, execute, and iterate on multi-step coding tasks — has already reached 30.9% adoption among developers. Meanwhile, METR's research shows that the task duration frontier AI agents can handle has been doubling every ~89 days since 2024. The shift from assistive completions to autonomous agents represents the next wave, but it also amplifies verification debt: the effort shifts from writing code to reviewing and debugging what the agent produced.

Sources: Stack Overflow 2025, METR Time Horizons (metr.org)
